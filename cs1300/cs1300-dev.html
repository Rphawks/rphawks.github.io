OVERVIEW

this project sought to use react development to create a simple filterable and sortable interface with mix-and-match components. in this case, an airline loyalty points calculator was made, which allows one to calculate the estimated cash equivalent of their loyalty points.

NOTE: I did not realize Spotify and Goodreads were used as examples in the handout until after doing the analysis…! Sorry!

For Spotify, they have one of the fastest ways to add a song to a favorites playlist by the simple click of a button, but they have the most convoluted removal process, as you cannot immediately undo the add action by clicking the same button or even another button on the same UI. Instead, navigation is required to move to the playlist page, where you can right click or hold to bring up a menu with a removal option. Similarly, finding more information about a song requires a right click to the same menu. Google Flights, in contrast to Spotify, has the easiest UI to navigate, allowing for a toggle to save or unsave a selection, a trash can to remove from the favorites list, and a button to step in for more information. It sorts searches by travel date, which makes sense for its use case, as users likely use travel date as part of their mental model of planning future travel. Goodreads shares many of these positives, but also has filtering, which can be useful for longer lists, and Goodreads allows for multiple favorites lists, all of which can be filtered. Goodreads uses “bookshelves,” which is an additional positive conceptual model.
